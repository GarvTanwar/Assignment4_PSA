Policies

Privacy Policy

This privacy policy explains what information Powering Tomorrow may receive through this website and how that information should be handled. The site is designed to collect as little personal information as possible.

Information we do not collect

Powering Tomorrow does not require visitor accounts, passwords, profiles, payment details, or personal dashboards. Visitors can read the campaign pages without signing in or submitting personal information.

The website is not intended to collect sensitive information such as identity documents, health information, home address details, or private student records.

Contact form information

If a visitor uses the contact form, the website may receive the name, email address, subject, and message that the visitor chooses to send. That information should only be used to read the message and respond to the enquiry.

Visitors should avoid sending private, sensitive, or unnecessary personal details through the contact form. If a message is sent by mistake, the sender can request that it not be used for any further response.

Technical information

Like most websites, basic technical information may be processed by the browser, hosting provider, or form service so that pages can load and messages can be delivered. This may include device type, browser type, approximate time of access, and standard server request information.

Powering Tomorrow does not use this website to build advertising profiles or sell visitor information.

Children and students

The campaign is suitable for families and school-related learning, but children should use contact features with parent, carer, or teacher support. Students should not submit personal school records, private addresses, or sensitive information through the website.

Keeping information limited

Any personal information received through direct contact should be kept only for as long as needed to respond to the message or manage the campaign. Information should not be shared publicly unless the sender has clearly given permission.

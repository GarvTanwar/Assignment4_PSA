Climate Change

Climate change is connected to the way energy is produced, used, and wasted. Families do not need to solve the whole problem alone, but everyday choices can help children understand why energy should be used carefully.

Climate learning works best when it feels practical, local, and calm. This page turns a large environmental issue into household routines that parents can explain and children can practise.

Household Energy

Why household energy matters

Homes use electricity for lighting, heating, cooling, cooking, screens, chargers, and appliances. When electricity is produced from fossil fuels, wasted energy can contribute to greenhouse gas emissions. Reducing waste is one practical way families can lower their impact.

Small actions are easier for children to understand when they are connected to daily routines. Turning off unused lights, closing doors while heating or cooling is running, and unplugging chargers are simple examples that make climate action feel real at home.

Parent Guidance

Teaching children without fear

Climate change can feel overwhelming, especially for young people. The campaign focuses on calm, practical habits instead of fear. Children learn best when adults model useful routines and explain the reason in simple language.

Energy is valuable, and using it wisely is one way families can care for the future.

At Home

What parents can do

01

Name the action

Say the habit out loud so children connect the action with its purpose: lights off, doors closed, chargers unplugged.

02

Make one child responsible

A child can check lights in one room or remind the family to unplug unused chargers after devices are full.

03

Compare the change

Families can look at usage before and after a routine change so children see that small habits can become visible results.

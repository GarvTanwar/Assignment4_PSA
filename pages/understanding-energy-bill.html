Understanding Your Energy Bill

An energy bill can help families understand how much electricity they use and when habits may be costing more than expected. Parents can use it as a practical teaching tool for older children.

What to look for

Most energy bills show the billing period, total usage, supply charges, usage charges, and the amount owed. Usage is often measured in kilowatt-hours, which represents how much electrical energy has been used over time.

Families can compare bills across months or seasons to see how heating, cooling, holidays, or new routines affect energy use.

Usage charges and supply charges

A usage charge is linked to the amount of electricity used. A supply charge is usually a daily cost for being connected to the electricity network. Understanding the difference helps families see which parts of the bill can be influenced by behaviour.

Turning off unused devices can reduce usage, but it will not remove fixed supply costs. This is why realistic expectations matter when teaching children about bills.

Using the bill as a family activity

Parents can show children a simple comparison: what changed this month, which appliances were used more, and which habits could improve next month. This makes energy-saving feel measurable instead of abstract.

Policies

Copyright & Usage

This page explains how Powering Tomorrow campaign material, images, text, and referenced sources should be credited and reused.

Campaign content

Original text, layout decisions, campaign messaging, page copy, and custom website presentation belong to the Powering Tomorrow campaign unless another source is clearly identified.

Visitors may refer to the campaign for personal study, school discussion, and educational review. Reuse should keep the campaign name attached and should not make the material look like it was created by another person or organisation.

Images, audio, and visual material

Images, campaign visuals, podcast cover material, icons, and audio-related content should not be reused as original work without permission or proper acknowledgement. If a visual is based on a third-party source, that source should also be respected.

Do not remove credits, crop out attribution, or reuse visuals in a way that changes the meaning of the campaign.

References and third-party information

The references page lists sources used for transparency. Facts, statistics, and external ideas should be credited to their original source when they are used in school work, presentations, or other projects.

Referencing Powering Tomorrow does not replace checking the original source. When accuracy matters, visitors should read the original publication and cite it directly.

Educational use

Teachers, students, and families may discuss or quote short parts of the campaign for learning purposes, provided the source is named and the material is not presented as someone else's original work.

For larger reuse, public posting, publication, or adaptation of the campaign materials, permission should be requested first.

Responsible attribution

A simple attribution can include the campaign name, page title, and website page used. For example, a student bibliography should identify Powering Tomorrow and the specific page consulted.

Any external source listed on the references page should be cited according to the citation style required by the teacher, school, or project.

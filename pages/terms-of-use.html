Policies

Terms of Use

These terms explain how visitors should use the Powering Tomorrow website and campaign material. The website is designed for education, awareness, and school project communication about household energy-saving habits.

Purpose of this website

Powering Tomorrow shares general information about reducing wasted energy at home. The content is written for parents, carers, students, and families who want simple ways to build better everyday habits.

The website does not provide professional energy, legal, financial, engineering, or safety advice. Visitors should check official government, school, energy provider, or qualified professional guidance before making decisions that affect electrical systems, household appliances, building changes, or personal circumstances.

Acceptable use

Visitors may read, share, and refer to this website for personal, educational, and classroom purposes. The site should not be used in a way that damages the website, interferes with another visitor, misrepresents the campaign, or presents campaign content as official government advice.

Any use of this website should remain respectful, lawful, and connected to education or awareness. Do not attempt to copy the website structure for misleading purposes, remove source acknowledgements, or use the campaign name to imply endorsement without permission.

Accuracy and updates

The campaign aims to present clear and useful information, but energy guidance can change over time. Facts, statistics, and source information should be checked against the references page and current official sources before being reused in formal work.

Powering Tomorrow may update, remove, or revise website content when information changes or when the campaign material is improved.

External sources and links

The website may refer to external organisations or public information sources. Those sources are included for transparency and further reading. Powering Tomorrow does not control external websites and is not responsible for changes to their content, privacy practices, or availability.
